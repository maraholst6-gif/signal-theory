# Signal Theory Quiz Questions - Framework Aligned

**Format:** All questions use the 4 signal states as answer options  
**Total:** 30 questions (10 beginner, 10 intermediate, 10 advanced)

---

## Standard Answer Template (Use for ALL questions)

**A) Interest** - Reciprocal effort, consistent forward movement, she initiates or follows through on plans  
**B) Uncertainty** - Inconsistent patterns, warm but distant, evaluating but hasn't decided  
**C) Comfort** - Easy connection but no romantic direction or escalation  
**D) Disengagement** - Moving away, fading energy, plans falling through, slower responses over time

## BEGINNER LEVEL (10 Questions)

### B1: Late Night Text
**Scenario:** She texts "Hey what are you up to?" at 11 PM, three days after your first date. No mention of the date or future plans.

**Question:** What signal state is this?

**Options:**
- A) Interest - She's showing reciprocal effort and forward movement
- B) Uncertainty - Inconsistent pattern, no clear forward direction ✓
- C) Comfort - Easy connection exists but no romantic escalation
- D) Disengagement - She's moving away with fading energy

**Correct Answer:** B (Uncertainty)

**Why:** Initiated contact is slightly positive, but late timing, no reference to the date, and three-day gap with no forward plans = uncertainty. She's checking in but not demonstrating clear romantic interest.

**Observable Behaviors:**
- Initiated contact (slight positive)
- Timing is late/casual (neutral)
- No reference to past date or future plans (neutral)
- Three-day gap since last contact (neutral)

**Common Mistake:** Interpreting late-night text as "she's thinking about me = interest." Timing and content matter more than just contact.

**Calibrated Response:** Reply warmly but don't over-invest. Keep it brief and friendly. Let her demonstrate more consistent interest before escalating.

**Framework Principle:** Signals Over Stories - Don't fill gaps with hopeful narratives. Observe behavior patterns.

### B2: App Match Goes Quiet
**Scenario:** Great conversation on a dating app for 2 days — responsive, funny, asking questions. Then mid-conversation, she stops responding. It's been 3 days.

**Question:** What signal state is this?

**Options:**
- A) Interest - She's showing consistent effort and forward movement
- B) Uncertainty - Inconsistent but still evaluating
- C) Comfort - Connection exists without romantic direction
- D) Disengagement - She's moving away, energy fading ✓

**Correct Answer:** D (Disengagement)

**Why:** Sudden silence after strong engagement = disengagement. Apps create parallel conversations; something else gained priority. The abrupt shift from engaged to silent is the key signal.

**Observable Behaviors:**
- Previous engagement was strong (context)
- Sudden silence mid-conversation (abrupt shift)
- No explanation or re-engagement after 3 days (absence of behavior)

**Common Mistake:** Thinking "maybe she's just busy" and sending multiple follow-ups. On apps, silence is common and rarely personal.

**Calibrated Response:** At most one brief follow-up ("Hey, still interested?"). If no response, move on. Don't send multiple messages.

**Framework Principle:** Signals Over Stories - Her absence IS the signal. Don't create hopeful explanations.

### I1: Delayed Response Pattern
**Scenario:** She used to reply within an hour. Over the past week, responses take 6-8 hours and her messages have gotten shorter — often one sentence or less.

**Question:** What signal state is this?

**Options:**
- A) Interest - Consistent engagement
- B) Uncertainty - Evaluating, inconsistent
- C) Comfort - Easy connection maintained
- D) Disengagement - Energy moving away ✓

**Correct Answer:** D (Disengagement)

**Why:** The directional change matters more than absolute timing. When energy consistently moves away (slower responses + shorter messages), that's disengagement.

**Observable Behaviors:**
- Response time increased significantly (directional change)
- Message length decreased (reduced investment)
- Pattern shifted from engaged to minimal (trajectory is away)

**Common Mistake:** Making excuses ("she's just busy this week") instead of reading the pattern.

**Calibrated Response:** Pull back. Stop investing where energy is shrinking. Match her level or below.

**Framework Principle:** The Master Question - Are you aligned with the signal (pulling back) or trying to change it (investing more)?

**Observable Behaviors:**

**Common Mistake:** Thinking she's "just shy" and needs more group hangouts to warm up.

**Framework Principle:** Signals Over Stories - Group-only availability is a boundary. Respect it.

### A10: Ex Reaches Out
**Scenario:** Ex-girlfriend (broke up 6 months ago) texts "Saw something today that reminded me of you. Hope you're doing well."

**Question:** What signal state is this?

**Options:**
- A) Interest - She wants you back
- B) Uncertainty - Could mean many things, unclear intent ✓
- C) Comfort - Friendly check-in from the past
- D) Disengagement - Just being polite

**Correct Answer:** B (Uncertainty)

**Why:** This could mean genuine care, testing availability, loneliness, guilt, or actual interest in reconnecting. The message alone doesn't clarify. Before analyzing her signals, ask: Are YOU over this?

**Observable Behaviors:**
- Initiated contact after 6 months (significant break in pattern)
- Nostalgic framing ("reminded me of you")
- Friendly but non-specific (no clear ask or direction)

**Common Mistake:** Either responding enthusiastically OR ignoring completely without first checking in with yourself.

**Calibrated Response:** Only engage if you're genuinely open to whatever comes next. Ask yourself what YOU want before responding.

**Framework Principle:** Signals Over Stories - Exes are a different category. Patterns from the relationship matter more than a single message.
