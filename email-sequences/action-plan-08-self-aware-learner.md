## Your 3 Blind Spots

**1. Awareness without consistent execution.**
You know what to do. You can even articulate it clearly. But under pressure — when she's sitting across from you and the moment calls for action — you sometimes default to old patterns instead of new ones. This isn't failure. It's the gap between intellectual understanding and embodied skill. A basketball player doesn't learn to shoot by understanding physics. He learns by taking ten thousand shots. You need reps, not more insight.

**2. Residual emotional weight from your past.**
You've processed a lot. But not all of it. There are moments — maybe specific triggers, maybe certain types of women, maybe particular dating scenarios — where old pain surfaces and influences your behavior. You notice it happening (that's the self-awareness), but noticing it doesn't always stop it. Some of this weight will lift with time. Some of it needs targeted attention. The key is knowing which is which.

**3. Inconsistent strategy application.**
You know what calibrated dating looks like. You can describe it. But you don't do it every time. Some dates, you're dialed in — relaxed, confident, reading signals well, escalating naturally. Other dates, you revert — overthinking, playing it too safe, or pushing too hard. The variance is narrowing (you're improving), but it's still there. Consistency is the last piece, and it's the piece that turns occasional good dates into a reliable pattern.

## Your Action Plan (This Week)

**1. Pick your #1 pattern to work on.** Not all three blind spots. One. The one that shows up most often. Maybe it's the over-texting when you get anxious. Maybe it's the hesitation when you should escalate. Maybe it's the comparison to your ex that surfaces on first dates. Name it specifically. "This week, I'm focused on [specific behavior]." Trying to fix everything at once is how you fix nothing.

**2. Create a pre-date ritual.** Before every date or interaction this week, take 2 minutes to set an intention. Not a vague one — specific. "Tonight, I'm going to let silences happen without filling them." "Tonight, I'm not going to check my phone for her response more than once per hour." "Tonight, I'm going to suggest the next date before we leave." One intention. Written down. Reviewed before you walk in.

**3. Do a 5-minute debrief after every interaction.** Not a spiral — a debrief. Three questions: (1) What did I do well? (2) Where did I revert to old patterns? (3) What would I do differently next time? Write it in your phone notes. This turns every date into a data point instead of a verdict. You're building a feedback loop that accelerates your improvement.

**4. Give yourself permission to be mediocre this week.** Seriously. You're in a learning phase. Putting pressure on yourself to be perfect is what causes the inconsistency — you try too hard, overcorrect, and spiral. Lower the bar to "show up and practice." That's it. The results will follow the reps. They always do.
