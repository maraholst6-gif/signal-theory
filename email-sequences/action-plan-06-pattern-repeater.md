## The Pattern You're In

You're stuck in a cycle, and it looks like this: You meet someone. There's instant chemistry — that "click" feeling, the sense that you just get each other. You dive in. Things move fast. And then, somewhere around week 4 to week 8, it falls apart in a way that feels eerily familiar. Because it IS familiar. You've been here before. Multiple times.

Here's what's actually happening: You're attracted to a type. Not a physical type — an emotional type. Women who are slightly unavailable. Women who run hot and cold. Women who give you just enough to keep you hooked but never enough to feel secure. This isn't bad luck. It's pattern recognition operating at a level you're not conscious of. Your nervous system is drawn to dynamics it recognizes — even when those dynamics are the exact ones that destroyed your last relationship.

This plays out in a few ways. Some guys are attracted to women who remind them of their ex — similar energy, similar communication style, similar emotional temperature. Others swing to the complete opposite: "She's nothing like my ex" becomes the primary filter, which is still a comparison, just inverted. And some are drawn to unavailable women specifically because the pursuit feels like love. The chase, the uncertainty, the highs and lows — that rollercoaster mimics emotional intensity, and your brain confuses it with genuine connection.

The deeper issue is what's driving the pattern. You're not just looking for a person. You're looking for an outcome. A relationship. Validation. Proof that you're worthy of love. That you can make it work this time. And that outcome-focus makes you rush. You skip the evaluation phase. You commit emotionally before you have enough information. You're so focused on the destination that you ignore every red flag on the road — because acknowledging them would mean starting over, and starting over feels unbearable.

So you stay. You invest. You try harder. And it ends the same way. Again.

## Your 3 Blind Spots

**1. The "type" you're attracted to is a trauma response.**
That instant chemistry you feel? It's not compatibility. It's recognition. Your nervous system is saying "I know this dynamic" and your brain is interpreting that familiarity as attraction. Healthy, available women don't trigger that same spark — because they represent something unfamiliar. Unfamiliar feels boring. But boring might actually be what healthy feels like, at first.

**2. You're fixated on outcomes, not connection.**
Ask yourself honestly: When you're dating someone, are you getting to know HER? Or are you evaluating whether she can become your girlfriend? There's a massive difference. Outcome fixation makes you skip past red flags, project qualities onto people, and invest before you have evidence that investment is warranted. You're not falling for people. You're falling for the idea of not being alone.

**3. You're rushing because sitting with uncertainty is painful.**
You move fast. First date to exclusive in three weeks. Texting all day by date two. Meeting friends by date four. This isn't enthusiasm — it's avoidance. Moving fast means you don't have to sit in the uncomfortable space of "I don't know where this is going." But that uncomfortable space is where real evaluation happens. By skipping it, you're committing to people you don't actually know.

## Your Action Plan (This Week)

**1. Write down the last 3 people you were seriously attracted to.** For each one, list: What drew you in? What was the dynamic like in the first month? How did it end? Look for the common thread. Not physical traits — emotional patterns. How they made you feel. What role you played. I guarantee you'll see the loop.

**2. Create a "slow down" rule.** No exclusivity conversations before date 6. No meeting friends before date 8. No daily texting before you've had at least 3 in-person dates. These numbers aren't magic — they're guardrails. They force you to stay in the evaluation phase long enough to actually see who someone is instead of who you want them to be.

**3. The "opposite test."** The next time you meet someone and feel zero instant chemistry — but she's kind, interesting, and available — go on a second date anyway. Not because you should force attraction. Because your "chemistry detector" is currently calibrated to dysfunction. Give healthy a chance to grow. It won't feel like fireworks. It might feel like peace. That's not a downgrade.

**4. Ask yourself one question before every date: "Am I excited about HER, or am I excited about not being alone?"** Be ruthless with the answer. If it's the second one, you're not ready for that date. Cancel it. The loneliness will still be there tomorrow, but at least you won't be dragging someone else into your process.
