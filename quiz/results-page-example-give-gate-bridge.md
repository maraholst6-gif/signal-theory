## Your Scores

**What this looks like:**
- You notice when you're comparing someone new to your ex
- You catch yourself when old patterns show up (chasing unavailable, moving too fast, etc.)
- You're honest with yourself about whether you're actually ready or just want to be ready

### Dating Strategy: LEARNING
Your approach is inconsistent—sometimes you're too passive, sometimes too eager. You don't have a clear framework yet, so every situation feels like you're winging it. But you're observing your own patterns, which is the first step to calibration.

**What this looks like:**
- You chase when you're excited, withdraw when you're unsure
- Every situation feels different and you're not sure what "normal" looks like
- You're trying to find the balance between interested and needy

## What This Means for You

**You're the ideal student.**

Most men struggle for months (or years) because they don't know what they don't know. They think they're reading signals correctly when they're not. They think they're ready when they aren't. They don't see their own patterns.

You do.

Self-awareness is 80% of the work. The other 20% is structured practice—seeing enough situations to recognize patterns quickly and respond calibrated instead of reactive.

**1. You catch yourself**
When you're about to over-invest, compare someone to your ex, or misread a signal—you notice. That pause is everything.

**2. You're coachable**
You're not defensive. You don't need to protect your ego. When someone points out a blind spot, you listen. That's rare.

**3. You're committed to improvement**
You took this quiz because you want to get better, not because you think you have it figured out. That growth mindset means fast progress.

## Where You'll Improve Fastest

Based on your profile, here's where you'll see the biggest gains:

**1. Signal Reading Accuracy**
Right now, you're aware you miss signals—but you don't know *which* signals you're missing or *why*. With structured practice, you'll start recognizing patterns:
- When politeness looks like interest (and when interest looks like politeness)
- When to make a move vs when to wait
- What "genuinely interested" actually looks like vs what your anxiety tells you

**2. Emotional Baseline**
You're in transition, which means some days you feel ready and other days you don't. The goal isn't perfection—it's stability. With more self-awareness, you'll know:
- When you're projecting vs seeing clearly
- When to take a step back vs push through
- What your actual readiness level is (not what you wish it was)

**3. Consistent Strategy**
Right now, your approach changes based on how you feel. The goal is a framework that works regardless of your mood:
- What calibrated looks like in different contexts
- How to respond to situations (not emotions)
- When to pursue, when to pull back, when to communicate directly

---

## What Happens If You Don't Address This

Here's the risk: You stay inconsistent.

You keep:
- Misreading some situations (and wondering why things fizzle out)
- Over-investing when excited, withdrawing when unsure (mixed signals to women)
- Winging it every time (exhausting and ineffective)

Eventually, one of two things happens:
1. You burn out from constant recalibration
2. You become cynical and stop trying

Neither helps you find what you're looking for.

But here's the good news: **You're not far from calibrated.** You're self-aware, coachable, and committed. That means you'll improve faster than most men.
