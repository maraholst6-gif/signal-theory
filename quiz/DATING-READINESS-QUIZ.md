# Your Dating Readiness Profile - Quiz

**Purpose:** Lead magnet quiz that diagnoses dating blind spots and creates desire for the app

**Format:** 15 questions, 3-4 minutes, real scenarios

**Output:** Personalized profile + blind spots + app CTA

---

## Quiz Questions

### Section 1: Signal Reading (5 questions)

**Q1: Match Response**
You match with someone attractive on Hinge. She responds to your opener with: "Haha yeah, I love hiking too!"

What do you do next?
- A) Ask her out immediately while she's engaged ✓ (Calibrated)
- B) Keep chatting to build more comfort first (Over-cautious)
- C) Send a longer message about your favorite hikes (Over-investing)
- D) Wait to see if she messages again first (Passive/testing)

**Scoring:**
- A = Signal reading: GOOD | Strategy: Active
- B = Signal reading: CAUTIOUS | Strategy: Passive
- C = Signal reading: POOR (misreading) | Strategy: Over-investing
- D = Signal reading: POOR (testing) | Strategy: Passive

---

**Q2: Response Time**
A woman you've been texting for 3 days takes 6 hours to respond. Before this, she was responding within 30 minutes.

What does this most likely mean?
- A) She's losing interest (Negative assumption)
- B) She's busy - it means nothing ✓ (Neutral/calibrated)
- C) She's testing me to see if I'll chase (Game assumption)
- D) I said something wrong (Self-blame)

**Scoring:**
- A = Signal reading: NEGATIVE BIAS (anxious)
- B = Signal reading: GOOD ✓
- C = Signal reading: GAME-FOCUSED (projecting strategy)
- D = Signal reading: SELF-FOCUSED (not reading her)

---

**Q3: Date Ending**
You had a great first date. At the end, she says "This was really fun!" and gives you a hug, but doesn't suggest meeting again.

What do you think?
- A) She's not interested - if she were, she'd suggest plans (Negative)
- B) Positive signal - follow up in a day or two ✓ (Calibrated)
- C) She's being polite but not into me (Misreading politeness)
- D) I should've kissed her - I missed my chance (Regret/second-guessing)

**Scoring:**
- A = Signal reading: POOR (requiring overt interest)
- B = Signal reading: GOOD ✓
- C = Signal reading: POOR (classic mistake)
- D = Signal reading: PRESSURE (forcing escalation)

---

**Q4: Text Conversation**
You've been texting back and forth. She asks what you do for work. You answer, then she doesn't respond for a few hours.

Your first thought:
- A) My answer was boring - I killed it (Self-blame)
- B) She's not interested anymore (Negative)
- C) She's probably just busy, I'll wait to hear back ✓ (Neutral)
- D) I should send another message to re-engage (Chase)

**Scoring:**
- A = Signal reading: SELF-FOCUSED (anxiety)
- B = Signal reading: NEGATIVE BIAS
- C = Signal reading: GOOD ✓
- D = Strategy: CHASE (over-investment)

---

**Q5: Plans She Made**
She suggests getting coffee "sometime next week." You offer Tuesday at 3pm. She says "I think I'm busy Tuesday, let me check my schedule and get back to you."

What's happening?
- A) She's brushing me off politely (Negative)
- B) She's actually busy - wait for her to suggest a time ✓ (Neutral/calibrated)
- C) She's low interest - high interest women make time (Negative)
- D) I should suggest 2-3 other options to make it easy (Over-pursuing)

**Scoring:**
- A = Signal reading: POOR (assuming rejection)
- B = Signal reading: GOOD ✓
- C = Signal reading: NEGATIVE BIAS (anxious)
- D = Strategy: OVER-PURSUING (chasing)

---

### Section 2: Emotional Readiness (5 questions)

**Q6: The Comparison**
You're on a date with someone new. She makes a joke that reminds you of something your ex used to say.

Your reaction:
- A) Feel a wave of nostalgia/sadness (Still attached)
- B) Notice the similarity but stay present ✓ (Moved on)
- C) Immediately compare her to your ex in your mind (Not ready)
- D) Feel annoyed that she reminded you of your ex (Unprocessed)

**Scoring:**
- A = Readiness: LOW (still grieving)
- B = Readiness: HIGH ✓
- C = Readiness: LOW (comparing)
- D = Readiness: LOW (unprocessed anger)

---

**Q7: Ex Contact**
Your ex texts you "thinking of you, hope you're well" out of the blue. You're seeing someone new casually.

Honest reaction:
- A) Feel excited/hopeful - maybe she wants to reconnect (Still attached)
- B) Feel annoyed - I've moved on ✓ (Detached)
- C) Feel confused - I don't know what I want (Ambivalent)
- D) Don't respond - I'm focused on new person ✓ (Moved on)

**Scoring:**
- A = Readiness: LOW (still attached)
- B = Readiness: HIGH ✓
- C = Readiness: MEDIUM (in transition)
- D = Readiness: HIGH ✓

---

**Q8: The Type**
The women you're most attracted to tend to be:
- A) Similar personality/look to my ex (Pattern repeating)
- B) Completely opposite of my ex (Reactive)
- C) Varies - I'm attracted to different types ✓ (Healthy)
- D) Emotionally unavailable or hard to read (Familiar pattern)

**Scoring:**
- A = Readiness: LOW (repeating pattern)
- B = Readiness: MEDIUM (reactive, but aware)
- C = Readiness: HIGH ✓
- D = Readiness: LOW (chasing familiar)

---

**Q9: Why Dating?**
If you're honest, you're dating because:
- A) I'm ready to meet someone new ✓ (Healthy)
- B) I'm lonely and want companionship (Avoiding grief)
- C) I want to prove I'm over my ex (Reactive)
- D) Everyone says I should start dating again (External pressure)

**Scoring:**
- A = Readiness: HIGH ✓
- B = Readiness: LOW (using dating to cope)
- C = Readiness: LOW (not ready)
- D = Readiness: LOW (not internally motivated)

**Q10: Relationship Talk**
A woman you've been seeing for a few weeks brings up "what are we looking for?"

Your honest feeling:
- A) Panic - too soon, I'm not ready (Avoidant)
- B) Comfortable - I'm open to seeing where this goes ✓ (Ready)
- C) Excited - I want a relationship now (Rushing)
- D) Confused - I don't know what I want (Not ready)

**Scoring:**
- A = Readiness: LOW (avoidant)
- B = Readiness: HIGH ✓
- C = Readiness: MEDIUM (rushing, but ready)
- D = Readiness: LOW (not clear)

### Section 3: Dating Strategy (5 questions)

**Q11: Multi-Dating**
Your approach to dating multiple people:
- A) I date one person at a time exclusively (Serial monogamy)
- B) I date multiple until someone wants exclusivity ✓ (Calibrated)
- C) I feel guilty dating multiple people (Monogamous wiring)
- D) I don't date multiple - if I'm interested, I focus (Outcome-focused)

**Scoring:**
- A = Strategy: SERIAL (not wrong, but limits options)
- B = Strategy: CALIBRATED ✓
- C = Strategy: GUILT (emotional blocker)
- D = Strategy: OVER-INVESTMENT (too fast)

---

**Q12: Investment Timing**
When do you start getting emotionally invested?
- A) After 1-2 great dates (Too fast)
- B) After sex (Common, but fast)
- C) After 4-6 dates when I see consistency ✓ (Calibrated)
- D) I don't invest until she shows clear interest (Reactive)

**Scoring:**
- A = Strategy: OVER-INVESTMENT (too fast)
- B = Strategy: SEX = CONNECTION (common error)
- C = Strategy: CALIBRATED ✓
- D = Strategy: PASSIVE/REACTIVE (waiting for proof)

---

**Q13: Initiating Contact**
Who texts first most of the time in your early dating experiences?
- A) Me - I initiate 80%+ of conversations (Over-pursuing)
- B) About 50/50 split ✓ (Balanced)
- C) Her - I wait to see if she reaches out (Passive/testing)
- D) Depends on how interested I am (Reactive)

**Scoring:**
- A = Strategy: CHASING (over-investment)
- B = Strategy: BALANCED ✓
- C = Strategy: PASSIVE (testing interest)
- D = Strategy: CONDITIONAL (inconsistent)

---

**Q14: First Move**
You've been on 2-3 dates. There's been no physical contact yet.

What do you do?
- A) Wait for a clear sign she wants me to make a move (Passive)
- B) Create an opportunity and gauge her response ✓ (Active/calibrated)
- C) Ask "would it be okay if I kissed you?" (Safe but low polarity)
- D) Go for a kiss at the end of the next date (Planned/forced)

**Scoring:**
- A = Strategy: PASSIVE (waiting)
- B = Strategy: CALIBRATED ✓
- C = Strategy: SAFE (not wrong, but removes tension)
- D = Strategy: FORCED (not reading the moment)

---

**Q15: Outcome Focus**
When you're early in dating someone, you find yourself:
- A) Thinking about where this is going / future scenarios (Outcome-focused)
- B) Enjoying the process and staying present ✓ (Process-focused)
- C) Analyzing every interaction for "good" or "bad" signs (Anxious/analytical)
- D) Not thinking about it much until it gets serious (Avoidant)

**Scoring:**
- A = Strategy: OUTCOME-FOCUSED (creates pressure)
- B = Strategy: PROCESS-FOCUSED ✓ (healthy)
- C = Strategy: ANXIOUS (over-analyzing)
- D = Strategy: AVOIDANT (disconnected)

## Scoring Matrix

### Dimension 1: Signal Reading Ability
- **Strong (8-10 good answers):** You read situations accurately
- **Developing (5-7):** You have blind spots around politeness/interest
- **Weak (0-4):** You're misreading signals regularly

### Dimension 2: Emotional Readiness
- **Ready (8-10 high):** You've processed the past, open to new
- **In Transition (5-7):** Some attachment/patterns still present
- **Not Ready (0-4):** Still attached, comparing, using dating to cope

### Dimension 3: Dating Strategy
- **Calibrated (8-10 good):** Balanced, present, not outcome-focused
- **Learning (5-7):** Some over-investment or passivity
- **Misaligned (0-4):** Chasing, passive, or anxious patterns

## Profile Types (8 profiles)

### Profile 1: "The Ready Navigator"
- Signal Reading: STRONG
- Readiness: READY
- Strategy: CALIBRATED

**Description:**
You're in great shape. You read situations accurately, you've processed your past, and your approach is balanced. You're not chasing, not waiting, not over-analyzing.

**What you need:**
Fine-tuning. You're ready to date successfully, but practicing scenarios in the app will help you recognize subtle patterns faster and avoid the few blind spots you still have.

**App focus:** Advanced scenarios, edge cases, pattern recognition

---

### Profile 2: "The Rusty Romantic"
- Signal Reading: WEAK
- Readiness: READY
- Strategy: CALIBRATED

**Description:**
You're emotionally ready and your approach is sound, but you're misreading signals. You're confusing politeness for interest, forcing clarity too early, or over-investing when the situation doesn't warrant it.

**What you need:**
Signal reading training. You're ready to date, but you need reps to re-learn modern dating communication (apps, texting, ambiguity).

**App focus:** Core Signal Theory scenarios, text/app-based situations

**CTA:** "You're ready, but you're flying blind. The app teaches you to read the room."

---

### Profile 3: "The Eager Rebuilder"
- Signal Reading: DEVELOPING
- Readiness: NOT READY
- Strategy: ACTIVE (too active)

**Description:**
You're jumping into dating before you're ready. You're still comparing people to your ex, using dating to cope with loneliness, or chasing familiar patterns. Your signal reading is shaky, and you're pursuing too hard.

**What you need:**
Slow down. Work through the "Moving On" scenarios first. Figure out why you're dating (healing vs readiness) before you invest more time in the wrong situations.

**App focus:** "Moving On" module, readiness self-assessment

**CTA:** "You're not ready yet - and that's okay. The app helps you figure out when you are."

---

### Profile 4: "The Cautious Observer"
- Signal Reading: STRONG
- Readiness: READY
- Strategy: PASSIVE

**Description:**
You read situations well and you're emotionally ready, but you're playing it too safe. You're waiting for overt interest, avoiding risk, or requiring too much certainty before you make a move.

**What you need:**
Permission to act. You know what's happening, you just need practice being more active. The app will show you when passivity kills momentum and when moving forward is calibrated.

**App focus:** First-move scenarios, escalation timing, risk calibration

**CTA:** "You see it clearly - now learn when to act on it."

---

### Profile 5: "The Wounded Analyst"
- Signal Reading: WEAK (anxious)
- Readiness: IN TRANSITION
- Strategy: ANXIOUS

**Description:**
You're over-analyzing everything, assuming negative signals, and self-sabotaging. You're not fully over your ex, and that anxiety is bleeding into every interaction. You're chasing reassurance instead of building attraction.

**What you need:**
Both emotional work AND signal training. Start with the readiness work, then move to scenarios. You need to separate your anxiety from reality.

**App focus:** "Moving On" module + signal reading basics

**CTA:** "Your anxiety is distorting reality. The app helps you see what's actually happening."

---

### Profile 6: "The Pattern Repeater"
- Signal Reading: DEVELOPING
- Readiness: NOT READY (repeating patterns)
- Strategy: OUTCOME-FOCUSED

**Description:**
You're attracted to the same type (or opposite extreme) as your ex. You're rushing toward outcomes (relationship, validation) instead of being present. You're not ready because you're unconsciously recreating familiar dynamics.

**What you need:**
Awareness of your patterns. Why are you attracted to unavailable women? Why do you need outcomes instead of process? The "Moving On" module will hit hard.

**App focus:** Pattern recognition, "Moving On" scenarios

**CTA:** "You're repeating the same pattern. The app breaks the cycle."

---

### Profile 7: "The Inconsistent Dater"
- Signal Reading: DEVELOPING
- Readiness: READY
- Strategy: REACTIVE/INCONSISTENT

**Description:**
You're ready emotionally and your signal reading isn't terrible, but your strategy is all over the place. You chase when interested, go passive when unsure, and can't find a consistent approach.

**What you need:**
A framework. You need Signal Theory's structured approach to replace your reactive, mood-based strategy. The app gives you a playbook.

**App focus:** Strategy scenarios, consistency training

**CTA:** "You're ready, but you need a plan. The app gives you one."

---

### Profile 8: "The Self-Aware Learner"
- Signal Reading: DEVELOPING
- Readiness: IN TRANSITION
- Strategy: LEARNING

**Description:**
You're aware of your blind spots, you're working through your past, and you're trying to find your footing. You're not perfect, but you're self-aware and committed to getting better. You're in the best position to improve.

**What you need:**
Reps and feedback. You know what you don't know. The app gives you structured practice with immediate feedback on where you're still struggling.
