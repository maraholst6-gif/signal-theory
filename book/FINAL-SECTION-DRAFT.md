### What This Looks Like in Practice

Let's walk through a real scenario using both sides of the framework.

**The Situation:**

You've been on two dates with someone. Both felt good. Conversation flowed. There was laughter. Physical touch happened naturally. You're interested.

After the second date, you text to say you had a great time. She responds warmly but doesn't suggest meeting again. A few days pass. You reach out to make plans. She's agreeable but vague about timing.

**What most men do:**

They assume uncertainty means disinterest. They either push for clarity ("Are you still interested?") or pull back entirely and wait for her to initiate.

Both moves create pressure.

**What Signal Theory tells you:**

**Step 1: Identify the signal state.**

This is uncertainty, not disengagement. She's responsive but not driving. Energy is present but not directional.

**Step 2: Check what you're sending.**

Are you broadcasting anxiety by over-texting or over-explaining? Are you leaning in too hard emotionally? Or are you staying calm and reciprocal?

If you're grounded, the uncertainty is hers to resolve. If you're anxious, you may be creating it.

**Step 3: Respond appropriately.**

Stay warm but don't chase. Suggest a specific plan. If she's vague again, give space.

Let the signal clarify itself without forcing it.

**Step 4: Stay regulated.**

Don't let her uncertainty destabilize you. Your emotional state is yours to manage.

If she moves toward you, great. If she doesn't, you haven't lost anything. You've gained information.
